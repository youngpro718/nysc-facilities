1. Getting Started & Onboarding

Creating an Account

  1. Navigate to the NYSC Facilities Hub login page.
  2. Click Create one at the bottom of the sign-in form.
  3. Fill out your First Name, Last Name, Email, and Password.
  4. Select your Account Type. This is critical, as it determines your default access level (see Roles & Permissions). Options include User, Court Aide, Court Officer, and Management.
  5. (Optional) Provide your Job Title, Phone Number, Department, Court Position, Room Number, and Emergency Contact.
  6. Click Create Account.

The Approval Process

Immediately after signing up, your account is placed into a Verification Pending state.

  • You will receive an email to verify your email address. Click the link in that email.
  • Once verified, an Administrator must manually approve your account and confirm your requested role in the Admin Center.
  • Attempting to log in before approval will redirect you to a "Pending Approval" screen.

Logging In

  1. Enter your registered email address and password.
  2. Click Sign in.
  3. If successful, you will be redirected to the Dashboard matching your approved role.

3. Dashboard & Navigation

The Dashboard provides a consolidated view of your facility's operational health.

Global Navigation

The Left Sidebar is your main navigation hub. At the top of every screen:

  • Search Bar (⌘K or Ctrl+K): Instantly search for spaces, people, or inventory items across the entire application.
  • Notification Bell: Displays active alerts, such as assigned tasks, pending approvals, or new high-priority issues.
  • Theme Toggle: Switch between Light Mode, Dark Mode, and System Default.
  • User Avatar: Click to access your Profile, modify your settings, or Sign Out.

Reading Data Cards

The top strip of the Dashboard contains live Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):

  • Active Issues (Red/Blue): Total number of unresolved issues. The red number indicates critical urgency.
  • System Health (Green/Yellow): A percentage metric calculating uptime across lighting, HVAC, and generic spatial readiness.
  • Tasks Pending: Staff tasks that are awaiting manager approval or assignment.
  • Maintenance Queue: A tally of scheduled and active maintenance jobs.

Building Grid

Each building tracked by the system (e.g., 100 Centre Street, 111 Centre Street) is represented by a card.

  • Health Ring: A visual donut chart of the building's operational status.
  • Quick Links: Clicking a building routes you to the Spaces module filtered precisely to that facility's rooms.

4. Spaces (Directory & Floor Plans)

The Spaces module is the master directory for every physical room in your managed facilities.

Searching and Filtering

  • Category Chips: Quickly filter the room list by clicking Offices, Courtrooms, Chambers, Storage, or Lockers.
  • Left Panel List: Displays room numbers, categories, and floors. A green status dot indicates "Operational." Amber or Red dots indicate pending maintenance or critical issues.

Viewing Room Details

Click any room in the left panel to open the Room Detail Panel on the right.

  • Room Lighting: Displays a dial of functioning fixtures (e.g., 85% healthy). Admins can manually override this value using the pencil icon if IoT integrations are offline.
  • Open Issues: Lists the exact tickets currently assigned against this specific room.
  • Assigned Personnel: Shows the names and titles of staff whose primary office or assignment is set to this location.

Adding a New Space

Requires Administrator or Management role.

  1. Click the + Add Space button in the top right.
  2. Enter the Room Number, Name, Building, and Floor.
  3. Select the Room Type (e.g., Courtroom, Holding Cell, Judicial Chamber).
  4. Set its initial operational status.
  5. Click Save Space. It immediately becomes available in searches and filters.

5. Issues & Operations

This module represents the ticketing and workflow engine for building maintenance.

The Issue Lifecycle

Every issue goes through a set progression:

  1. Submitted / Active: A user submits a ticket. It appears in the blue "Active" column.
  2. In Progress: A Court Aide or Maintenance worker claims the ticket by clicking "Start Work" from the ⋮ menu. It moves to the orange column.
  3. Resolved: Once the actual physical work is verified, the worker selects "Mark Resolved." It moves to the green "Resolved Today" column and is archived the following day.

Creating an Issue

  1. Click + Report Issue.
  2. Select an Issue Type (e.g., Plumbing, Electrical, HVAC, Structural).
  3. Select the specific Location (Building and Room).
  4. Provide a detailed Description.
  5. Set the Priority (Low, Medium, High, Critical). Critical issues trigger system-wide notification alerts to Management.
  6. Upload any relevant photos (if applicable).
  7. Submit the report.

Scheduling Maintenance

Maintenance differs from Issues; it is proactive rather than reactive.

  • Navigate to the Maintenance tab.
  • Click Schedule Maintenance.
  • Define the recurrence (e.g., Weekly HVAC filter checks), assigning personnel and due dates.

6. Key Management (Inventory & Assignments)

A highly secure module explicitly designed for Court Officers to track physical access control.

Lockbox Management

Digital lockboxes centralize physical keys.

  1. Go to the Lockbox tab.
  2. Select a Lockbox (e.g., "Main Security Desk").
  3. Use + Add Key Slot to register a new hook or peg in the box.
  4. Linking a Key: Click an empty slot to map it to a specific Key Category (e.g., "Master Courtroom Key").

Issuing a Key to a User

  1. Navigate to the Assign tab.
  2. Select the Key Category from the dropdown.
  3. Select the physical Key Copy / RFID Tag from inventory.
  4. Search for the Personnel member receiving the key.
  5. Set the Checkout Time and an optional Expected Return Time.
  6. Type any specific notes (e.g., "Temporary issue for painting contractor").
  7. Click Confirm Assignment. The key will move from "Available" to "Assigned" status.

Auditing (Key Rounds & History)

  • The History tab acts as an immutable ledger, recording every single checkout and return timestamp, along with the authorizing Officer.
  • Print Lockbox Reference (🖨️): Generates a paper-ready checklist of a lockbox for physical End-of-Shift reconciliation rounds.

7. Access & Personnel

The personnel directory connects human resources to physical spaces and keys.

Navigating the Directory

  • Department Filtering: Narrow down the list by choosing terms like "Supreme Court", "Court Security", or "Administration".
  • Status Pills: "Has Rooms" and "Has Keys" filters quickly isolate individuals who hold physical facility assets.

Personnel Detail Cards

Clicking any individual reveals:

  • Their title and primary department.
  • Room Assignments: A list of offices or courtrooms exclusively linked to them.
  • Active Keys: A live view of any physical keys currently in their possession.

Adding New Personnel

  1. Click + Add Personnel.
  2. Enter their name, job title, and internal department code.
  3. This creates their Personnel Record. (Note: This is separate from their User Login Account; a personnel record simply tracks their physical assets, whether they interact with the software or not).

8. Inventory & Storage

Manage consumable stock across the entire court ecosystem—from printer toner to cleaning supplies.

Tracking Low Stock

  • The Overview dashboard highlights "Low Stock Items" in red.
  • You can configure custom minimum thresholds (e.g., alert when Copy Paper drops below 10 boxes).

Fulfilling Supply Requests

When standard users order supplies, they land here.

  1. Click the Requests button (noting the red notification badge for pending orders).
  2. Review the incoming request details, including the Requestor and Delivery Location.
  3. Verify physical inventory in the respective Storage Room.
  4. Click Approve & Fulfill. Doing so will automatically deduct the requested quantities from your tracked system inventory.
  5. Click Reject if the item is out of stock or unauthorized, providing a reason note back to the user.

Storage Rooms

Track where items live.

  • The Storage Rooms tab lists closets and supply depots.
  • You can see exact locations (e.g., Room 104-B) and print out a manifest of all items currently housed in that specific room.

9. Tasks & Workflows

Tasks are structured assignments, differing from "Issues" because they are top-down directives rather than bottom-up reports.

Creating a Directive

  1. Click + Create Task.
  2. Title the task (e.g., "Restock all 3rd-floor bathrooms with paper towels").
  3. Set the Category (Routine, Delivery, Inspection).
  4. Set the Assignee (route it directly to a specific Court Aide or Maintenance Staff member).
  5. Add deadline dates and priority tags.

Managing Workflow

  • Pending vs Active: A task starts as "Pending". Once the assigned user begins working, they click "Start," moving it to "Active."
  • If a task requires verification, the user will submit it for approval, notifying the manager who originally created it. The manager can then "Complete" or "Reject" the work.

10. Lighting Management

Monitor and maintain the illumination health of massive, multi-story buildings.

The Two Metrics

  • Room Lighting: The total sum of healthy fixtures located inside closed rooms.
  • Hallway Lighting: The total sum of healthy fixtures in public corridors (critical for safety and security).

Troubleshooting

  • The system flags "Critical Issues" for completely blacked-out areas.
  • Floor View: Toggle the display to visualize lighting health segmented by building floors, making it easy to identify localized electrical failures vs single burnt-out bulbs.

11. Court Operations

A quick-glance portal designed specifically for building executives to understand the day's tempo.

Daily Snapshot capabilities

  • Sessions Today: Pulls data on scheduled litigation and hearings.
  • Staff Out: Cross-references personnel records to highlight missing Judges or essential security staff.
  • Room Issues: Flags any courtroom that has an active "Critical" issue tying it up (e.g., broken HVAC rendering the space unusable).
  • Conflicts: An algorithmic check that alerts you if a room is double-booked or if a scheduled room is simultaneously marked "out of service" by facilities.

12. Admin Center (Settings & Approvals)

The engine room of the application, restricted to Users holding the "Administrator" role.

Approving and Managing Users

  1. The User Management screen lists all registrants.
  2. New signups appear here with a Verification Status.
  3. Click the Role Dropdown on any user's card to alter their permissions immediately.
  4. Use the ⋮ menu on the right edge to deactivate terminated employees or force a password reset workflow.

Advanced System Settings (Routing & Templates)

  • Routing Rules: Configure automation. For example, instruct the system: "If an Issue is tagged 'Plumbing', automatically assign it to the Maintenance Team Lead."
  • Form Templates: Customize the exact questions asked when a user creates an Issue or orders a supply. You can add required text boxes, dropdown selections, or file upload requirements.
  • Key Requests: Review and formally approve requests for permanent key issuance submitted by staff.

13. Supply Requests

A streamlined consumer-style ordering experience for court personnel.

Making an Order (The Cart Workflow)

  1. Go to the Supply Room or Request Hub.
  2. Search for your item (e.g., standard pens, a new chair). Use category pills for faster filtering.
  3. Click Add to move the item into your persistent shopping cart at the bottom of the screen.
  4. Click the + or - buttons to adjust quantities.

Restricted Items & Justification

  • Items marked with an Amber Triangle Triangle (⚠️) are "Restricted" (e.g., expensive electronics or furniture).
  • If your cart contains a restricted item, the system will prevent a "Quick Submit."
  • You must open the cart and fill out the mandatory Justification text box explaining why you need the item before the Submit Order button illuminates.

Tracking Your Order

You can track orders directly from your personal dashboard. Orders pass through these states:

  • Submitted: Awaiting review by the Inventory Team.
  • Approved: The Inventory Team has accepted the order and is gathering materials.
  • Fulfilled: The supplies have been delivered to your location.
  • Rejected: The order was denied with an explanatory note.
